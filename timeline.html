Journey Through Hindu India's History

Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE)

The Bronze Age civilization known for advanced urban planning, drainage systems, and early Hindu symbols like the Swastika and Pashupati seal.

Vedic Period (1500–500 BCE)

Composition of the Vedas, the oldest scriptures of Hinduism. Development of early Hindu rituals and the caste system.

Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE)

Under Ashoka, Buddhism flourished but Hindu traditions continued. Development of stone architecture and edicts.

Gupta Empire (320–550 CE)

The "Golden Age" of Hindu culture with advancements in art, science, and literature. Development of classical Hindu temple architecture.

Medieval Period (550–1200 CE)

Construction of magnificent temples like Khajuraho and Konark. Development of Bhakti movement emphasizing devotion to deities.

Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526 CE)

Islamic rule brought new influences, but Hindu traditions persisted. Syncretic styles emerged in art and architecture.

Mughal Empire (1526–1857 CE)

Despite Islamic rule, Hindu art flourished in miniature paintings and regional kingdoms maintained Hindu traditions.

Colonial Period (1757–1947 CE)

British rule led to Hindu reform movements. Revival of classical arts and emergence of modern Hindu nationalism.

Independent India (1947–Present)

Hinduism adapts to modern world while maintaining traditions. Global spread of Hindu philosophy and yoga.
